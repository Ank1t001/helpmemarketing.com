Most ad spend doesn't fail. It leaks.

The campaigns are running. The dashboards say things look fine. But the cost per customer keeps climbing, and no one can say exactly why.

Meta CPAs rose 149% in the US between 2024 and 2025. CPMs rose 81%. CPC rose nearly 19%. Click cost is up. Customer cost is up much faster. The gap between the two is where the leak lives.

This is what we see in almost every audit:

  • Rising costs, flat revenue. Reach is more expensive every quarter. Conversion volume isn't keeping pace. Most teams react by adding spend instead of asking why the unit economics are deteriorating.
  • Attribution holes. iOS 17 broke pixel-only tracking for half of mobile traffic. Server-side, CAPI, and consent-aware events are now table stakes. Most accounts still report off the old data.
  • Wrong-fit leads. Optimization for cheap clicks fills your funnel with people who never convert. Sales teams quietly stop following up. The data looks fine on the platform dashboard. The business sees no result.
  • No incrementality view. Spend that would've happened anyway gets credit for "driving" sales. Without holdout testing, you can't tell which channels actually lift the business and which ones just take credit.

Most agencies optimize the wrong layer of this stack. They tune bids, swap creatives, and chase platform metrics. The leak stays.

We start at the bottom.

How we run paid media that actually compounds.

Most performance marketing engagements start the same way: pick the channels, build the campaigns, launch fast, optimize later. The result is a portfolio that looks active but never compounds. Spend goes up. Returns don't.

We work in the opposite order.

1. Start with measurement, not the channel.

Before we touch a campaign, we rebuild the data foundation. Server-side tracking, CAPI, consent-aware events, GA4 properly configured, and a clear attribution model your team can defend. Most accounts skip this because it's invisible work. Then they spend the next two years arguing with platform-reported numbers that don't match revenue.

2. Build for cost-per-outcome, not platform metrics.

Every channel optimizes for what you tell it to. Tell Meta to find clicks, you get clicks. Tell it to find customers, you get customers. We structure each campaign around the actual unit economics, so the platform learns to find buyers who match your margin, not just users who happen to click.

3. Iterate on the lever that moves the number.

Creative is the only variable left once measurement and structure are right. We run weekly creative tests, kill what doesn't work, double down on what does, and feed audience and platform signals back into the build. The account doesn't just maintain. It compounds.

What we run.

Four channels, each with a different job in your funnel. We start where the unit economics work and expand from there.

Google Ads

Capture demand that's already searching for what you sell.

What we run

  • Search campaigns built around intent. Keyword structures that match how your buyers actually search, not how the keyword tool groups them.
  • Performance Max with guardrails. PMax can find revenue or waste budget. We constrain it with audience signals, asset specificity, and reporting that exposes what's actually working.
  • Demand Gen and YouTube. For brands ready to build new demand, not just capture existing search.
  • Shopping feeds tuned for margin. Product titles, GTIN coverage, and feed segmentation aligned to which SKUs you actually want to scale.
  • Negative keyword discipline. Most accounts bleed 10-20% to irrelevant queries. We audit and prune weekly.

Clients consolidate scattered spend, recover budget from wasted impressions, and grow the queries that actually convert.

Meta Ads

Build demand and scale buyers across Facebook and Instagram.

What we run

  • Advantage+ structured for your business. Meta's automation works when it has clean signal. We feed it server-side events, clear conversion definitions, and creative variation it can learn from.
  • Creative-as-lever testing. Weekly velocity on hooks, formats, and offers. Most accounts fail here because they run the same 3 ads for 6 months.
  • Audience structure that doesn't fight the algorithm. Lookalikes, interest stacks, and exclusions built to give Meta room to find buyers, not boxed into manual targeting that limits scale.
  • Retargeting that actually returns. Sequenced flows that match buyer intent stage, not the same generic "come back" ad to everyone. For brands running automated follow-up sequences, we sync the retargeting strategy with the post-purchase flow.
  • Server-side CAPI from day one. Pixel-only attribution lost half its signal after iOS 17. We rebuild it properly.

Clients see consistent buyer acquisition cost, recover lost attribution signal, and run creative at the velocity Meta's algorithm actually rewards.

LinkedIn Ads

Reach decision-makers in B2B markets where every other channel guesses.

What we run

  • Account-based targeting at scale. Build target account lists from your CRM, then deploy company-level and persona-level campaigns against them.
  • Sponsored content for thought leadership. Distribute the content your team is already creating to the audience that actually hires you.
  • Lead Gen Forms tuned for sales handoff. Native LinkedIn forms with the right fields, the right qualifier questions, and the right CRM integration so sales gets context, not just an email.
  • Conversation Ads for high-intent flows. When you need a decision-maker to engage with a specific offer, not just see your name.
  • Cost-per-lead discipline. LinkedIn is expensive. We optimize for cost-per-qualified-lead, not cost-per-form-fill.

Clients turn LinkedIn from a brand-awareness vanity buy into a measured pipeline channel with attributable revenue.

TikTok Ads

Reach buyers where attention is, with content that earns it.

What we run

  • Spark Ads and creator content. Native-feeling ads built around real creators, not polished corporate spots that get scrolled past.
  • Audience signals tuned to TikTok's algorithm. Interest categories, behavioral signals, and creator audience targeting layered for performance.
  • Pixel and event setup that survives signal loss. Server-side events and TikTok's Events API for accurate conversion tracking.
  • Creative testing at TikTok velocity. TikTok demands new content constantly. We build the creative engine to keep up.
  • Cost-per-purchase optimization, not cost-per-view. TikTok will spend your budget on cheap impressions if you let it. We make sure it doesn't.

Clients enter TikTok with creative and measurement built for performance, not chasing trends that don't convert.

Built into every engagement: Attribution & MMM.

Channels are the menu. Attribution is the operating system underneath them.

Every engagement includes server-side tracking setup (Google Ads Enhanced Conversions, Meta CAPI, TikTok Events API, LinkedIn Insight Tag), GA4 configured properly, consent-aware event taxonomy, and a clear attribution and measurement model your team can defend in a board meeting.

Where it adds value, we run quarterly incrementality tests (geo holdouts, channel pauses, lift studies) and Marketing Mix Modeling to answer the question platform dashboards can't: which channels are actually driving the business, and which are just taking credit?

What engagements look like in practice.

We don't sell tiers. Every paid media engagement is scoped to your business, your margin structure, and the channels that match your funnel. But the patterns repeat. Here's what HMM engagements typically look like:

A growth-stage DTC brand

The situation: A 7-figure DTC brand running Meta and Google in-house. CPAs climbing month over month, attribution scattered across three different reporting tools, creative refresh cycle slower than the algorithm needs.

What we build: Full attribution rebuild (CAPI, server-side events, GA4 cleanup), Meta restructured around margin-aware optimization, weekly creative testing cadence, Google PMax constrained with proper signal feeds. Channel handoff between Meta and Google mapped to the actual buyer journey.

The shift: CPA stabilizes. Reporting becomes a single source of truth. Creative velocity matches algorithm requirements. The team shifts from firefighting platform changes to scaling what works.

A B2B SaaS

The situation: A B2B SaaS company with a small marketing team running LinkedIn and Google Search. Pipeline coming in but unclear which channels are driving qualified opportunities versus form-fills that never close.

What we build: Account-based LinkedIn campaigns targeting CRM lookalikes and target account lists, Google Search optimized for commercial-intent keywords, Lead Gen Forms restructured for sales-team handoff, and attribution rebuilt to track from ad click through to closed-won revenue.

The shift: The team stops debating which channel "works" and starts seeing which one delivers pipeline that converts. Spend gets reallocated to the channel actually moving revenue, not the one with the cheapest CPL.

A multi-location service business

The situation: A regional service business (healthcare practices, legal, home services) with 5-15 locations. Google Search running but campaigns blur across locations, PMax burning budget on irrelevant queries, no clear view of which locations need more spend.

What we build: Location-segmented Google Search campaigns with location-specific landing pages, PMax constrained by location and asset specificity, Google Business Profile integration, call tracking tied to revenue, and a reporting layer that shows per-location performance.

The shift: Each location becomes its own optimization unit. Budget moves toward locations with capacity to grow. The business stops over-spending in mature markets and under-spending in launch markets.

A consumer brand entering TikTok

The situation: A consumer brand (apparel, beauty, food & beverage, lifestyle) with a working Meta program looking to scale into TikTok. Worried about creative requirements, unsure if the audience converts, no measurement foundation built for TikTok specifically.

What we build: TikTok Pixel + Events API setup, Spark Ads program with sourced creator content, audience structure tuned for TikTok's algorithm (not ported from Meta), creative testing cadence built for TikTok velocity, and reporting that ties TikTok spend to attributed revenue rather than vanity engagement.

The shift: TikTok stops being an experiment and becomes a measured acquisition channel. Creative output matches platform expectations. The brand reaches buyers Meta and Google can't.

Frequently asked questions.

What's the minimum ad spend to work with you? +

It depends on the channel mix and what you're trying to learn. For most engagements, the build cost makes sense when monthly paid media spend is in the $6K-$10K range. Below that, we'd recommend running campaigns yourself with platform-direct support, or starting with an Ad Account Audit before committing to a full build.

How long until I see results? +

Foundational work shows up in 30-45 days (attribution rebuild, campaign restructure, initial creative testing). Compounding results show up in 90-120 days as the algorithm learns, creative wins surface, and the optimization layer matures. Anyone promising faster is either lying or skipping the measurement work that makes the results last.

Do you take a percentage of ad spend? +

No. We charge a flat monthly fee scoped to the engagement. Percentage-of-spend models incentivize agencies to scale spend regardless of returns. We'd rather get paid the same whether your spend goes up or down, so the recommendation we give you is the one that's actually best for your business.

Do you work with my existing team or replace them? +

We work alongside. Most engagements involve us running the strategy, attribution, and channel structure while your team handles creative input, brand voice, and customer feedback. For brands without an internal marketing team, we run end-to-end. Either way, we don't keep the work secret. You own the accounts, the data, and the playbook.

What if I just want an audit, not a full engagement? +

Then start with the audit. We run a 45-minute deep dive on your existing accounts, surface where the spend is leaking, and give you a written recommendation. No commitment to a full engagement. About a third of audit clients implement the recommendations themselves; about two-thirds bring us in to execute. Either is fine.

Who this isn't for.

Honest qualifiers. Performance marketing isn't the right fit for every business, and we'd rather tell you upfront than waste a discovery call.

You're spending under $6K/month with no plan to scale. Below that, the cost of building proper measurement and the velocity needed for paid media optimization doesn't pay back. Run campaigns yourself with platform-direct support, or start with an Ad Account Audit before committing to a build.

You need leads in the next 30 days. Paid media compounds. The first 30-45 days go to measurement, structure, and creative foundation. Optimization gains show up after that. If you need leads next week, paid ads on shortcut deployment will burn budget. Buy a list, run cold outreach, or call existing customers instead.

You're not willing to share revenue and attribution data. Optimization for cost-per-outcome requires knowing what closes, not just what clicks. If we can't see the revenue side of your funnel, we can't tell Meta or Google what success actually looks like for your business. We can sign an NDA. We can't fly blind.

You want guaranteed ROAS. No legitimate paid media operator can guarantee specific returns. Algorithms change, competitors enter markets, creative fatigues, attribution shifts. We commit to disciplined execution, measurement honesty, and weekly iteration. Anyone promising more than that is telling you what you want to hear.
