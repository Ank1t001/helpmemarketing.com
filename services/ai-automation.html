Most customers prefer messaging. Most businesses still don't.

The way customers want to talk to businesses has changed. The way most businesses talk back hasn't.

75.1% of consumers want to message businesses like they do friends and family. 72.4% are more likely to buy from brands that offer messaging. They want WhatsApp, chat, and SMS, not phone trees and email forms.

But messaging only works at speed. A first response time of more than 5 minutes reduces the likelihood of conversion by 65%. Most businesses can't reply that fast. Not because they don't want to, but because no human team can.

This is where most businesses lose:

  • You lose the insight. Every WhatsApp, chat, and SMS lives in someone's phone. No transcripts, no patterns, no data. You can't improve what you can't see.
  • You lose the customer. They message at 9 PM. You reply at 10 AM. They've already bought from someone else. WhatsApp delays cause purchase abandonment for 56% of users.
  • You lose consistency. Different staff answer the same question different ways. Some reps quote outdated pricing. Some forget to mention the warranty. Your brand inherits all of them.
  • You lose growth. Every new customer adds the same workload. Scaling means hiring more people to give different answers to the same questions.

Most businesses know this. Few do anything about it because the alternative (building real AI automation) looks too expensive, too technical, or too slow.

We disagree.

How we build AI that actually works.

Most AI automation projects fail the same way. A generic chatbot gets deployed, makes customers angrier than they were before, and quietly gets switched off six months later. We build differently.

1. Start with your data, not a template.

The fastest path to a useless AI is to drop a generic chatbot on your site. We start by analyzing your actual customer transcripts and team responses, the way a new employee would learn the job. Your AI inherits the voice, the answers, and the edge cases your team already handles.

2. Build into your tools, not around them.

Most chatbots live in isolation. They answer questions, then dead-end the conversation when something needs to happen in another system. We integrate yours into your CRM, calendar, ticketing, and ops stack from day one. The AI doesn't just talk to customers. It books appointments, updates records, and routes complex requests to the right person on your team.

3. Measure conversations, not just deployments.

Going live isn't the win. Performance is. We track replies sent, response times, conversion rates, escalation patterns, and resolution time from week one. Every conversation becomes data you can act on. The AI gets better. So does the team using it.

What engagements look like in practice.

We don't sell tiers. Every AI build is scoped to your business, your tools, and your customer volume. But the patterns repeat. Here's what HMM engagements typically look like:

A multi-location service business

The situation: A growing service business with 4-6 locations, missing 25-40% of inquiries because no one's at the front desk after 6 PM or on weekends.

What we build: Voice AI Agent that answers every call, books appointments directly into the calendar, handles routine questions in the brand's voice, and escalates complex requests to the right location.

The shift: Customers stop leaving voicemails that go unreturned. Bookings happen at the moment of intent, not the next business day.

A DTC e-commerce brand

The situation: A growing DTC brand drowning in WhatsApp and email inquiries (order status, return requests, product questions) handled by a small team that can't scale.

What we build: WhatsApp AI Agent that handles 70-80% of customer service end-to-end (orders, returns, FAQs, payment confirmations), plus automated follow-up sequences for cart abandonment and post-purchase nurture, paired with their paid acquisition flow.

The shift: Response times go from hours to seconds. The team shifts from answering "where's my order?" to handling complex cases that actually need a human.

A B2B SaaS

The situation: A B2B SaaS company with a small sales team. Website visitors arrive curious, but most leave without engaging. Inbound leads sit in a queue until someone has time to qualify them.

What we build: Website Chatbot that engages visitors based on what they're reading, captures qualified leads with full context, and routes them into the CRM with sales-ready signals.

The shift: Passive visitors become qualified conversations. The sales team works on warm leads instead of cold ones.

A high-volume consumer business

The situation: A consumer business (hospitality, events, services) handling 500+ customer touchpoints per week across phone, WhatsApp, email, and chat. Different staff give different answers. No data on what customers are actually asking.

What we build: Multi-channel AI agents (Voice + WhatsApp + Chat) trained on the brand's actual responses, integrated with the booking system, plus a reporting layer that surfaces what customers ask most.

The shift: One consistent voice across every channel. Patterns become visible. The business stops being surprised by what its customers actually want.

Frequently asked questions.

How long does it take to launch? +

Most builds go live in 2-4 weeks. The exact timeline depends on how many tools we integrate with and how much training data you have on hand. Simple Voice AI or WhatsApp deployments can ship in under 2 weeks. Multi-channel builds with custom workflows typically take 3-4. We give you a concrete date after the first discovery call.

What does pricing look like? +

Pricing depends on scope, integrations, and ongoing involvement. A single agent build runs differently from a multi-product stack with custom workflows. We quote per project after we understand your customer volume, your tools, and what you actually need automated. No hidden retainers, no licensing markup on AI usage.

Will the AI sound robotic to my customers? +

No, if it's built right. The reason most chatbots sound robotic is that they're trained on generic templates, not your actual business. We train on your team's real conversations, your tone, your edge cases. Customers don't think they're talking to AI. They think they're getting a fast, accurate response. Which is the point.

What happens when the AI can't handle something? +

It escalates to a human on your team with full context. The AI doesn't pretend to know things it doesn't. When a request is outside scope (a complex complaint, a custom quote, a sensitive issue), the conversation transfers to the right person with the full transcript so they can pick up without making the customer repeat themselves.

How is this different from off-the-shelf chatbots? +

Off-the-shelf chatbots are designed for the average business. Yours isn't average. Generic chatbots use template responses, work in isolation from your actual tools, and break the moment a customer asks something specific. We build AI trained on your real conversations, integrated with your real systems, and tuned weekly based on real customer interactions.

Who this isn't for.

Honest qualifiers. AI automation isn't the right fit for every business, and we'd rather tell you upfront than waste a discovery call.

You're handling fewer than 50 customer touchpoints a month. AI economics make sense at scale. If you're getting a handful of customer questions a week, hire a part-timer or keep doing it yourself. The build cost won't pay back.

You don't use any CRM, calendar, or ticketing tool. The AI integrates with the tools your business already runs on. Without an operational foundation, there's nothing to connect the AI to. Build the basics first, then come back.

You want AI to replace your sales team, not amplify it. AI handles routine questions, books appointments, and routes complex requests to humans. It doesn't close deals, build trust, or sell. If you're trying to eliminate your team rather than free them up, this isn't the service.

You need it live next week. Real builds take 2-4 weeks minimum. Templated "instant" chatbots exist if speed is the only requirement, but you came here because you wanted custom-built. That takes time.
